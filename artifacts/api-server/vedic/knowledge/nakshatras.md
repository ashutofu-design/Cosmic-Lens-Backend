# Nakshatras — The 27 Lunar Mansions

The zodiac is divided into 27 nakshatras of 13°20′ each. The Moon's nakshatra at birth (Janma Nakshatra) is the single most important factor in Vedic predictive astrology after the Lagna. Each nakshatra has a ruling planet (which sets the Vimshottari dasha sequence), a presiding deity, a symbol, a gana (temperament), a yoni (animal nature) and a varna (caste).

## The 27 Nakshatras

### 1. Ashwini (0°00′ – 13°20′ Aries) — Ketu
- **Deity**: Ashwini Kumaras (twin physicians of the gods)
- **Symbol**: Horse's head
- **Gana**: Deva (divine)  •  **Yoni**: Horse (male)  •  **Varna**: Vaishya
- **Nature**: Swift, healing, pioneering, impatient. Natural healers, surgeons, athletes, riders. Quick-start energy but may not finish.
- **Career**: medicine, surgery, defence, sports, automobile, transport.
- **Marriage**: independent partner needed; conflict if controlled.

### 2. Bharani (13°20′ – 26°40′ Aries) — Venus
- **Deity**: Yama (god of death/dharma)
- **Symbol**: Yoni / vulva (creative-receptive womb)
- **Gana**: Manushya  •  **Yoni**: Elephant (male)  •  **Varna**: Mleccha
- **Nature**: Passionate, sensual, transformative, restraint required. Carries karmic burden of births and deaths.
- **Career**: arts, entertainment, midwifery, hospice, transformation work, food, hospitality.

### 3. Krittika (26°40′ Aries – 10°00′ Taurus) — Sun
- **Deity**: Agni (fire god)
- **Symbol**: Razor / flame / axe
- **Gana**: Rakshasa  •  **Yoni**: Sheep (female)  •  **Varna**: Brahmin
- **Nature**: Sharp, cutting, purifying, brilliant. Critical mind, leadership through fire. Krittika people see through pretense.
- **Career**: military, surgery, criticism, journalism, cooking (Agni), priesthood.

### 4. Rohini (10°00′ – 23°20′ Taurus) — Moon
- **Deity**: Brahma / Prajapati
- **Symbol**: Cart / chariot / temple
- **Gana**: Manushya  •  **Yoni**: Serpent (male)  •  **Varna**: Shudra
- **Nature**: Most beautiful and fertile nakshatra. Magnetic, sensual, materially blessed. Moon is exalted here.
- **Career**: agriculture, fashion, art, luxury goods, hospitality, dairy.
- **Marriage**: very fertile; brings wealth to spouse.

### 5. Mrigashira (23°20′ Taurus – 6°40′ Gemini) — Mars
- **Deity**: Soma (Moon-god) / Chandra
- **Symbol**: Deer's head
- **Gana**: Deva  •  **Yoni**: Serpent (female)  •  **Varna**: Farmer/Shudra
- **Nature**: Searching, curious, restless, gentle. Always seeking — knowledge, places, partners.
- **Career**: research, travel, writing, real-estate scouting, exploration.

### 6. Ardra (6°40′ – 20°00′ Gemini) — Rahu
- **Deity**: Rudra (storm form of Shiva)
- **Symbol**: Teardrop / diamond
- **Gana**: Manushya  •  **Yoni**: Dog (female)  •  **Varna**: Butcher
- **Nature**: Stormy, transformative, sharp intellect, prone to grief. Destroys to renew.
- **Career**: science, IT, electricity, demolition, weather, psychology, foreign tech.

### 7. Punarvasu (20°00′ Gemini – 3°20′ Cancer) — Jupiter
- **Deity**: Aditi (cosmic mother)
- **Symbol**: Quiver of arrows / bow
- **Gana**: Deva  •  **Yoni**: Cat (female)  •  **Varna**: Vaishya
- **Nature**: Renewal, return of light, abundance, protective. Lord Rama born here.
- **Career**: teaching, philosophy, publishing, hospitality, motherhood/childcare.

### 8. Pushya (3°20′ – 16°40′ Cancer) — Saturn
- **Deity**: Brihaspati (guru of gods)
- **Symbol**: Cow's udder / lotus / arrow
- **Gana**: Deva  •  **Yoni**: Goat (male)  •  **Varna**: Kshatriya
- **Nature**: Most auspicious nakshatra for material undertakings (Pushya = "to nourish"). Faith, dharma, prosperity.
- **Career**: politics, religion, finance, dairy, ministry. Pushya days excellent for new ventures.

### 9. Ashlesha (16°40′ – 30°00′ Cancer) — Mercury
- **Deity**: Naga (serpents)
- **Symbol**: Coiled serpent
- **Gana**: Rakshasa  •  **Yoni**: Cat (male)  •  **Varna**: Mleccha
- **Nature**: Hypnotic, intuitive, secretive, kundalini-related. Can heal or poison.
- **Career**: psychology, occult, espionage, pharmacy, snake-handling, deep research.
- **Caution**: gandanta zone (Cancer-Leo cusp) — last pada is karmically heavy.

### 10. Magha (0°00′ – 13°20′ Leo) — Ketu
- **Deity**: Pitris (ancestors)
- **Symbol**: Royal throne
- **Gana**: Rakshasa  •  **Yoni**: Rat (male)  •  **Varna**: Shudra
- **Nature**: Royal, ancestral, traditional. Connected to lineage karma. Past-life royalty.
- **Career**: government, judiciary, ceremony, ancestral business, museums.

### 11. Purva Phalguni (13°20′ – 26°40′ Leo) — Venus
- **Deity**: Bhaga (lord of wealth & enjoyment)
- **Symbol**: Front legs of a bed / hammock
- **Gana**: Manushya  •  **Yoni**: Rat (female)  •  **Varna**: Brahmin
- **Nature**: Pleasure, romance, leisure, creativity, marriage. The "honeymoon" nakshatra.
- **Career**: entertainment, romance industry, hospitality, fashion, music.

### 12. Uttara Phalguni (26°40′ Leo – 10°00′ Virgo) — Sun
- **Deity**: Aryaman (lord of contracts/friendship)
- **Symbol**: Back legs of a bed / four legs of a cot
- **Gana**: Manushya  •  **Yoni**: Cow (male)  •  **Varna**: Kshatriya
- **Nature**: Steady, contractual, friendly, sustained partnership. Marriage success.
- **Career**: contracts, law, NGO, charity, alliances, partnerships.

### 13. Hasta (10°00′ – 23°20′ Virgo) — Moon
- **Deity**: Savitar (form of Sun, motivator)
- **Symbol**: Hand / fist
- **Gana**: Deva  •  **Yoni**: Buffalo (male)  •  **Varna**: Vaishya
- **Nature**: Skillful hands, craftsmanship, manifesting, witty, healing touch.
- **Career**: surgery, crafts, massage, palmistry, comedy, programming, jewellery making.

### 14. Chitra (23°20′ Virgo – 6°40′ Libra) — Mars
- **Deity**: Tvashtar (celestial architect, Vishwakarma)
- **Symbol**: Bright jewel / pearl
- **Gana**: Rakshasa  •  **Yoni**: Tiger (female)  •  **Varna**: Farmer
- **Nature**: Brilliant, beautiful, designing, charismatic, magnetic.
- **Career**: design, architecture, fashion, jewellery, photography, cinema.

### 15. Swati (6°40′ – 20°00′ Libra) — Rahu
- **Deity**: Vayu (wind god)
- **Symbol**: Young shoot blowing in wind / coral
- **Gana**: Deva  •  **Yoni**: Buffalo (female)  •  **Varna**: Butcher
- **Nature**: Independent, adaptive, business-minded, diplomatic, scattered.
- **Career**: business, trade, diplomacy, aviation, yoga, wind-energy.

### 16. Vishakha (20°00′ Libra – 3°20′ Scorpio) — Jupiter
- **Deity**: Indra-Agni
- **Symbol**: Triumphal archway / potter's wheel
- **Gana**: Rakshasa  •  **Yoni**: Tiger (male)  •  **Varna**: Mleccha
- **Nature**: Goal-driven, achievement-oriented, competitive, multi-tasker.
- **Career**: politics, sports, sales, military, spiritual leadership.

### 17. Anuradha (3°20′ – 16°40′ Scorpio) — Saturn
- **Deity**: Mitra (lord of friendship)
- **Symbol**: Lotus / staff
- **Gana**: Deva  •  **Yoni**: Deer (female)  •  **Varna**: Shudra

### 18. Jyeshtha (16°40′ – 30°00′ Scorpio) — Mercury

### 19. Mula (0°00′ – 13°20′ Sagittarius) — Ketu

- **Caution**: Mula gandanta — first pada most karmic; some traditions recommend Mula-shanti puja.

### 20. Purva Ashadha (13°20′ – 26°40′ Sagittarius) — Venus

## Three Gandantas (knot points)

| Gandanta | Last pada of | First pada of |
|----------|--------------|---------------|
| Water-Fire | Revati (Pisces) | Ashwini (Aries) |
| Water-Fire | Ashlesha (Cancer) | Magha (Leo) |
| Water-Fire | Jyeshtha (Scorpio) | Mula (Sagittarius) |

A Moon in gandanta zone at birth produces karmic challenges (especially Mula-Jyeshtha for father, Ashlesha-Magha for mother, Revati-Ashwini for self). Specific shantis (Mula-shanti, Jyeshtha-shanti, Gandanta-shanti) are prescribed within the first 27 days after birth in classical texts.

## Pada (Quarter) Significance

Each nakshatra is divided into 4 padas of 3°20′ each, totalling 108 padas across the zodiac (matching mala beads). Each pada falls into one of the 12 navamsha (D9) signs. Pada determines:

- The Navamsha sign of the planet
- Sub-lord influence
- Specific behavioural shade within the nakshatra

Nakshatra-pada combinations are essential for accurate name selection (Akshara-naming), muhurat, and marriage matching (the Janma Nakshatra-pada is checked in detailed Kundli Milan beyond the 8-fold koota).

## Marriage Matching (Ashtakoot Reference)

Of the 8 kootas, the nakshatra of bride and groom drives:
- **Varna** (1 pt) — varna of nakshatra
- **Vashya** (2 pts) — animal/control category by sign
- **Tara** (3 pts) — distance count (1-9) between nakshatras
- **Yoni** (4 pts) — animal compatibility from nakshatra
- **Graha-Maitri** (5 pts) — friendship of nakshatra-lords
- **Gana** (6 pts) — Deva/Manushya/Rakshasa
- **Bhakoot** (7 pts) — Moon-sign distance
- **Nadi** (8 pts) — Adi/Madhya/Antya nadi (Vata/Pitta/Kapha)

Total = 36; ≥18 acceptable, ≥24 good, ≥28 excellent. Same-Nadi (especially Adi-Adi) is a major dosha requiring remedy or chart-level compensation.

## Use of Nakshatra in Daily Predictions

- **Janma Tara** (1st from birth nakshatra) — body, health
- **Sampat Tara** (2nd) — wealth gains
- **Vipat Tara** (3rd) — danger
- **Kshema Tara** (4th) — wellbeing
- **Pratyak Tara** (5th) — obstacle
- **Sadhaka Tara** (6th) — accomplishment
- **Vadha Tara** (7th) — enemy
- **Mitra Tara** (8th) — friendship
- **Param Mitra Tara** (9th) — best friend

When the daily transiting Moon enters a Tara position, that day's quality for the native is shaped accordingly.
